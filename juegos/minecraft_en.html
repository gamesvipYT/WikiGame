Minecraft is a sandbox game where players can explore a block-generated world, build structures, and survive against enemies. It allows solo or multiplayer gameplay.

Main Features

  • Unlimited block building
  • Exploration of infinite worlds
  • Game Modes: Survival, Creative, Adventure
  • Online multiplayer

History and Lore

Minecraft started in 2009 as "Cave Game", created by Markus "Notch" Persson. It quickly evolved, reaching alpha release in 2010 and official release in 2011.

Over time, dimensions (Nether, End), bosses like the Ender Dragon, villages, biomes, and mobs were added, giving players narrative depth and freedom.

Today, it remains one of the best-selling games worldwide, with constant updates, mods, and an active community.

Gameplay

Minecraft is an open-world game with no defined ending (though "The End" provides credits after defeating the Ender Dragon). Gameplay revolves around exploring, mining, and building with blocks.

Structures like villages, temples, and fortresses exist, along with possibilities to create mechanisms and farms. Difficulty is configurable, from Peaceful to Hardcore.

Minecraft Realms

Released in 2013, Realms offers easy-to-use private servers. Supports custom maps, mods (in some editions), and cross-platform play since 2016.

Customization

The modding community has created thousands of mods, textures, and custom maps that expand gameplay, from simple visual changes to complete new mechanics.
